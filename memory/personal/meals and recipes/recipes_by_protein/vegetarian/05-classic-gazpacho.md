# Classic Gazpacho

**Prep:** 20 min | **Chill:** 2 hours | **Serves:** 6
**Source:** Adapted from Love & Lemons (https://www.loveandlemons.com/gazpacho-recipe/)

> Cold Spanish tomato soup. Best in summer when tomatoes are at their peak. The 2-hour chill is non-negotiable — the flavors need time to meld. The soup actually gets better on days 2 and 3, so leftovers are a feature, not a bug.
>
> Authentic Andalusian gazpacho uses bread as a thickener; this version skips the bread for a lighter, brighter soup. If you want a thicker, creamier texture, soak 1 slice of white bread in water for 5 minutes and toss it in the blender with everything else.

---

## Ingredients
- 3 lbs ripe tomatoes (about 6 medium) — any variety; Roma are classic
- 1 English cucumber, peeled and roughly chopped (reserve ¼ cup finely diced for garnish)
- 1 red bell pepper, roughly chopped (seeds removed)
- ½ small red onion, roughly chopped (rinse under cold water to tame sharpness)
- 2 cloves garlic, smashed
- 1 small Fresno chile or jalapeño, seeds removed (optional — adds gentle heat; omit for kid-friendly)
- ⅓ cup extra-virgin olive oil
- 3 tbsp red wine vinegar (or sherry vinegar)
- 1 tsp honey (optional — rounds out acidic tomatoes)
- 1 tsp salt, plus more to taste
- ½ tsp black pepper
- ¼ cup fresh cilantro (optional — not traditional but adds a fresh note)

**For garnish (optional):**
- Reserved diced cucumber
- Diced tomato
- Drizzle of olive oil
- Small basil leaves or extra cilantro
- Crusty bread or grilled cheese on the side

---

## Directions

1. **Prep the tomatoes.** Core and roughly chop. If using very large or watery tomatoes, halve and squeeze out the seeds over the sink (less watery soup).
2. **Rough chop** the cucumber, bell pepper, onion, garlic, and chile (if using). Don't worry about uniform pieces — they're going in the blender.
3. **Blend in batches** if needed. Add tomatoes, cucumber, bell pepper, onion, garlic, chile, olive oil, vinegar, honey, salt, pepper, and cilantro to a blender. Puree on high for 60–90 seconds until smooth and vibrant red.
4. **Taste and adjust.** Add more salt, vinegar, or olive oil as needed. Soup should be tangy, savory, and balanced.
5. **Strain (optional).** For a silkier texture, pour through a fine-mesh strainer, pressing with a spatula. Skip this if you like it rustic.
6. **Chill.** Transfer to a sealed container and refrigerate at least 2 hours (overnight is even better).
7. **Serve.** Ladle into bowls. Top with reserved diced cucumber, diced tomato, drizzle of olive oil, and herb leaves. Serve with crusty bread, grilled cheese, or grilled ham and cheese.

---

## Notes / Variations
- **Tomato swap:** If tomatoes aren't great, use 2 lbs fresh + 1 (28-oz) can San Marzano tomatoes, drained.
- **Heat level:** 1 Fresno = mild-medium. 1 jalapeño = mild. Skip both for no-heat kid version.
- **Make-ahead:** Soup keeps 3 days refrigerated. The flavor deepens daily.
- **Pairs with:** Grilled ham & cheese, crusty bread, a simple green salad, or grilled shrimp on top.
- **Don't freeze** — texture breaks when thawed.
