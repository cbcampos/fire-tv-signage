# 🍣 Teriyaki Salmon Rice Bowls

**Serves 6**

## 🍚 Rice Base

### Ingredients

- 3 cups cooked jasmine or short-grain white rice (about 1¼ cups dry)
- Pinch of salt

### Instructions

1. Rinse rice until water runs clear. Cook with 2¼ cups water and a pinch of salt.
2. Bring to a boil, reduce to low, cover, and simmer 15 minutes. Rest 5 minutes, then fluff.

## 🍣 Teriyaki Salmon

### Ingredients

- 2¼ lbs skinless salmon fillets, cut into 1-inch cubes
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- Salt and pepper
- ⅓ cup soy sauce (low-sodium)
- 3 tbsp honey
- 2 tbsp rice vinegar
- 1 tbsp brown sugar
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp grated fresh ginger (or ½ tsp ground)
- 1 tbsp cornstarch + 2 tbsp cold water (slurry)

### Instructions

1. Pat salmon cubes dry. Season lightly with salt and pepper.
2. Whisk soy sauce, honey, rice vinegar, brown sugar, sesame oil, garlic, and ginger in a small bowl. Set aside.
3. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high. Sear salmon 2–3 minutes per side until golden.
4. Pour teriyaki sauce over salmon. Bring to a gentle simmer.
5. Stir cornstarch slurry and drizzle into the sauce. Stir 1–2 minutes until thickened and glossy.
6. Remove from heat.

## 🥗 Bowl Toppings (set out family-style)

- 3 cups steamed broccoli florets
- 1 English cucumber, sliced thin
- 2 avocados, sliced
- 1½ cups shredded carrots
- 1½ cups shelled edamame (steamed)
- 2 green onions, sliced (garnish)
- 1 tbsp sesame seeds (garnish)
- Optional: sriracha, extra teriyaki sauce, lime wedges

## 🍽️ Assemble

1. Scoop rice into bowls.
2. Top with teriyaki salmon and any extra sauce from the pan.
3. Arrange broccoli, cucumber, avocado, carrots, and edamame around the salmon.
4. Garnish with green onions and sesame seeds. Serve with sriracha or lime if desired.
